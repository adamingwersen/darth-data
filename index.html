1. What enables Big Data and why?

Why collect data? (consumer)

  • Better Products:
    • Usability by integration with other services
    • Backups of pictures, videos etc.
    • More features
    • Personalized recommendations

Why collect data? (provider)

  • Required for training of AI
  • It disencourages customers from leaving
  • Ad revenue

How is it collected?

By logging behaviour and actions

When you send a message or like a photo etc.

..but also when you're not actively providing input

Example: 2018 Study of Google

On systems such as Android, Chrome, Maps etc.:

2/3 of all data collected didn't require any input!

Source: Google Data Collection, Douglas C. Schmidt, Vanderbilt University

Trends

  • Users are more active online, and thus provide more information
  • More data is being collected pr. user pr. second
  • In addition more users are accessing the internet worldwide

3. The Death of Moore's Law

Moore's Law #1

States that the number of transistors on a chip would double every 2 years

..which has been the case for the past ~50 years

..but signs that it's coming to and end are showing

Source: Progress in Digital Integrated Electronics, Gordon E. Moore (1975)

Moore's Law #2

According to Intel it's proving increasingly difficult to uphold

The cost of decreasing transistor size has skyrocketed the past years

transistors have become so small ... that simple physics began to block the process

Quantum Mechanics limit the shrinkage of transistors, and we're around the threshold

Source: The Verge (2018), Chaim Gartenberg

Implications

  • Computation will not become increasingly efficient as previously observed
  • This may drive down the price of cutting edge processors
  • ..as competitors are catching up to Intel

5. The actual scale of server computing

The actual scale of server computing #1

More data is collected/analysed than ever before

Most communication handled on internet

Storage and analysis handled by data centers/server farms

.. how so?

The actual scale of server computing #2

  • 2013: U.S. Data Centers consumed 91B KwH annually
    • equivalent to the annual output of 34 large (500-megawatt) coal-fired power plants
  • 2020 Forecast: 140B KwH annually
    • ..that's the annual output of ~50 large power plants
    • ..costing $13B annually, in electricity bills
    • ..100M tonnes of carbon pollution

Source: Pierre Delforge 2015, NRDC

The actual scale of server computing #3

These numbers may not mean much by themselves

140B KwH was consumption of electricity in Poland, 2014

.. a country of almost 40M people.

Source: Worldbanks Databank
