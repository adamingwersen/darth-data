1. What enables Big Data and why?

Why collect data? (consumer)

  • Better Products:
    • Usability by integration with other services
    • Backups of pictures, videos etc.
    • More features
    • Personalized recommendations

Why collect data? (provider)

  • Required for training of AI
  • It disencourages customers from leaving
  • Ad revenue

How is it collected?

By logging behaviour and actions

When you send a message, upload a photo, consume media etc.

..but also when you're not actively providing input

Example: 2018 Study of Google

On systems such as Android, Chrome, Maps etc.:

2/3 of all data collected didn't require any input!

Source: Google Data Collection, Douglas C. Schmidt, Vanderbilt University

Evolution in data types and -size

  • With social media came explosion in images and short videos
  • With streaming came explosion in HD movies and HQ Music
  • ..and it's all being backed up and analyzed

Data creation

  • People connected to internet increasing by ~2%/yr

  • There are now more connected devices than people
  • IoT devices forecasted to over 20B by 2020
  • They all transmit or collect data

Source: International Telecommunications Union

Source: Independent, 2014

Source: State of IoT 2018

Trends summary

  • Users are more active online, and thus provide more information
  • More data is being collected pr. user pr. second
  • Number of devices capable of collecting data rapidly increasing
  • In addition more users are accessing the internet worldwide
  • Data is becoming larger and more frequently uploaded/downloaded

2. Data Hungry Algorithms

Early AI

  • Theoretical foundations of Neural Networks, late 1940s
  • Fell short compared to other techniques
  • Research resurged throughout 1970s, 1980s and halted
  • Not enough data nor computing power

AI in the 2000s

  • Neural Network research once again resurged
  • For the first time, the computers weren't the problem
  • Companies such as Google started investing heavily
  • Facebook emerged with a completely data-driven profit model

AI Today

  • Neural Networks are better than humans at recongnizing objects in images
  • Deployed in all aspects of automated systems
  • Today used for a wide variety of tasks
  • The use is rapidly increasing and applications widening

Neural Networks #1

Neural Networks' performance depend on vast amounts of data

..they can often make sense of data, that humans can't comprehend

..and spot patterns that humans would never see

Neural Networks #2

Neural Networks use convolution

Which means, that the algorithm crops, scales, distorts and rotate input

This is very expensive from a computational standpoint

..and one of the main reasons, why NN's only recently have become successful

Trends Summary

  • Algorithms are becoming more complicated
  • Thus, more computationally expensive
  • Require more data to keep improving performance

3. The Death of Moore's Law

Moore's Law #1

States that the number of transistors on a chip would double every 2 years

..which has been the case for the past ~50 years

..but signs that it's coming to and end are showing

Source: Progress in Digital Integrated Electronics, Gordon E. Moore (1975)

Moore's Law #2

According to Intel it's proving increasingly difficult to uphold

The cost of decreasing transistor size has skyrocketed the past years

transistors have become so small ... that simple physics began to block the process

Quantum Mechanics limit the shrinkage of transistors, and we're around the threshold

Source: The Verge (2018), Chaim Gartenberg

Implications

  • Computation will not become increasingly efficient at previously observed rate
  • The rate at which data needs processing is outpacing the increase in processing power performance increases

4. The actual scale of server computing

The actual scale of server computing #1

More data is collected/analyzed than ever before

Most communication handled on internet

Storage and analysis handled by data centers/server farms

.. how so?

The actual scale of server computing #2

  • 2013: U.S. Data Centers consumed 91B KwH annually
    • equivalent to the annual output of 34 large (500-megawatt) coal-fired power plants
  • 2020 Forecast: 140B KwH annually
    • ..that's the annual output of ~50 large power plants
    • ..costing $13B annually, in electricity bills
    • ..100M tonnes of carbon pollution

Source: Pierre Delforge 2015, NRDC

The actual scale of server computing #3

These numbers may not mean much by themselves

140B KwH was consumption of electricity in Poland, 2014

.. a country of almost 40M people.

Source: Worldbanks Databank

The actual scale of server computing #4

In 2017: 3% of Global Power Consumption by data centers

Forecast: 20% of GPC by 2025

Source: Forbes, 2017

Source: Total Consumer Power Consumption Forecast, Anders Andrae, Huawei

5. Future, remedies and business opportunites

Future

Dramatic increases in power consumption by data centers

More firms will become self-reliant (solar, wind, hydro, etc.)

Increased awareness among researchers, governments and consumers

Need for green transition or de-escalation of Big Data Procedures

Remedies

Legislative interference

Consumers demanding green practices

Exogenous shock to energy prices

Alternative services

Current Profit Model

Consumers pay for some service with their data

Data is used to create profiles about costumers

Precise profiles are valuable to e.g. advertisers

Business Opportunites (Alternative Services)

  • Localized data transactions: Peer-to-peer
  • Service that tracks data throughput and caps it
  • Subscription-based model to opt-out
